\begin{abstract}
Three research lines developed in parallel now admit a common structural formalization:
they have been unified in a machine-checked Lean~4 development at the abstract
architecture level, with zero proof placeholders.  The NEMS program proves that load-bearing determinacy cannot
be outsourced: if a framework is non-categorical under closure, some internal selector is
forced, and in diagonal-capable settings that selector cannot collapse to a total-effective
decider on the relevant self-referential fragment.  The APS program proves that indexed
composition is neither automatic from self-reference nor a vague closure property: it is
exactly finite tracking plus gluing, so fixed-point abundance alone does not suffice for
global uniform realization.  Infinity Compression proves that canonical theorem-level
certification does not exhaust reflective structure: enriched realization above the bare
certificate is irreducibly plural, and any adequate certification route must instantiate
split/refinement/fiber architecture.

This paper presents the machine-checked synthesis of these three lines as the Strata
program.  NEMS governs the \emph{outer admissibility law}; APS governs the \emph{exact
local-to-global architecture required for uniform realization}; Infinity Compression
governs the \emph{inner reflective geometry} of canonical certification.

The formalization proceeds in three milestones, all clean.  \textbf{Milestone~1}
(abstract architecture and anti-collapse): defines an abstract class $\RAL$, proves a
layered architecture theorem showing that all three structural conclusions hold jointly, and
proves a stratified non-collapse theorem showing the three layers resist simultaneous
flattening.  \textbf{Milestone~2} (first bridge theorems --- one separation theorem, one interaction
theorem, one independence-plus-joint-packaging theorem): proves that bare canonicity does not pin reflective
finality; that a gluing--route coherence predicate connects middle tracking/gluing to the
inner residue chain under canonical certification; and that inner enriched gap is
independent of outer theory extent while holding jointly with outer semantic
non-exhaustion under the full layered hypotheses.  \textbf{Milestone~3} (non-erasure
summit): strengthens the architecture class with two explicit cross-layer coherence
axioms and proves a complete package of non-erasure results, culminating in the
\textbf{Non-Erasure Principle}: under the Milestone~3 layered and coherence hypotheses and
a remainder-bearing internal theory, enriched irreducibility holds; and the \textbf{Unified
Non-Erasure Law}: under the coherence axioms and universal totality, $\EnrIrr \iff \exists
T,\,\SemRem(T)$.  This is the abstract architecture-level unifying law corresponding to the NEMS
semantic remainder theorem and the IC enriched residue theorem.

The summit result is: in any architecture in $\RAL$ satisfying the Milestone~3 layered
and coherence hypotheses and carrying a remainder-bearing internal theory, outer semantic
non-exhaustion, inner enriched irreducibility, and the full inner residue package hold
simultaneously and resist joint flattening.  Concrete discharge of these abstract results
into the NEMS, APS, and IC source corpora is the remaining open step.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The present paper is a synthesis paper, and its central claims have been machine-checked
as abstract synthesis theorems inside the class $\RAL$.  Three parallel research lines ---
NEMS, APS, and Infinity Compression --- have been unified in the Lean~4 repository
\url{reflexive-architecture-lean} with zero proof placeholders across all three milestones
of the formalization.  Concrete discharge of the abstract results into the source corpora
is a separate remaining task, discussed in Section~\ref{sec:conjecture}.

\subsection{The three lines}

Taken separately, the NEMS, APS, and Infinity Compression lines appear to answer
different questions.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{NEMS:} What must be true of a system that claims to determine its own
realized world without importing ontologically external selector structure
\cite{SpivackNEMS01-2026,SpivackNEMS08-2026}?
\item \textbf{APS:} What is the exact local-to-global architecture required to pass from
local representability and fixed-point phenomena to global uniform composition --- rather
than relying on fixed-point abundance alone \cite{SpivackAPS2026}?
\item \textbf{Infinity Compression:} What remains of reflective structure once
canonical theorem-level certification has already been achieved \cite{SpivackIC2026}?
\end{enumerate}

Taken together, they answer a single deeper question:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{What is the structure of a genuinely reflexive foundation?}
\end{quote}

A closed foundational system is not merely a system with self-reference, fixed points,
or a canonical theorem.  It is a system in which:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item determinacy is internally borne rather than outsourced,
\item the machinery of realization contains the exact local-to-global architecture
required to pass from local representability to uniform composition, rather than
relying on fixed-point abundance alone,
\item successful certification still leaves an irreducible reflective overstructure
rather than collapsing to a fully exhausted point,
\item and the connection between outer semantic remainder and inner enriched
irreducibility is itself a provable law under explicit cross-layer coherence hypotheses.
\end{enumerate}

This synthesis establishes a three-layer architecture:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Layer & Governing line & Structural role \\
\midrule
$\Outer$ & NEMS & Admissibility of determinacy under closure \\
Middle & APS & Exact mechanics of uniform realization \\
$\Inner$ & Infinity Compression & Reflective residue above canonical certification \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{What is new}

The thesis that these layers form a unified architecture has been machine-checked as an
abstract synthesis theorem in the class $\RAL$, in three milestones.  Milestone~1
establishes the abstract class and an anti-collapse theorem.  Milestone~2 proves the first
bridge results across layers.  Milestone~3 proves the non-erasure summit: a biconditional law connecting outer
semantic remainder with inner enriched irreducibility, which is the abstract unifying
principle behind both the NEMS and IC results.  All results carry zero proof placeholders.
Concrete instantiation from the source corpora into $\RAL$ is the next step, described
at the end of Section~\ref{sec:conjecture}.

The reason Milestone~3 is the summit is that it is the first point at which the outer
and inner strata are no longer merely co-present or bridged locally, but are connected
by a full biconditional law inside $\RAL$.

\paragraph{Scope.}
All results in this paper are proved inside $\RAL$ at the abstract architecture level.
They do not yet constitute concrete imported instances of the NEMS, APS, and IC source
corpora, nor do they show that the Milestone~3 coherence axioms follow automatically
from those corpora.  The contribution is an abstract machine-checked synthesis with
proved bridge and non-erasure laws internal to $\RAL$, plus toy architectures witnessing
consistency of the design.  The strongest new result is the Unified Non-Erasure Law
(Section~\ref{sec:formalization}): under the added coherence axioms and universal
totality, outer semantic remainder and inner enriched irreducibility are equivalent
inside $\RAL$.

\subsection{Milestone 1: layered architecture and stratified non-collapse}

The outer layer is abstracted by \texttt{ReflexiveLayer}, supporting a generic theorem
of the form
\[
\SemRem(T)\ \lor\ \neg\, \mathrm{TotalOn}(T)
\]
for every internal self-theory $T$ under the barrier hypothesis — this is what we call
\emph{semantic non-exhaustion}.  The middle layer is abstracted by
\texttt{RealizationLayer}, carrying the exact composition characterization
\[
\Comp \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \FinTrack \wedge \Glue.
\]
The inner layer is abstracted by \texttt{CertificationLayer}, with an implication chain
from canonical bare certification and adequacy to split, route necessity, strict
refinement, and fiber nontriviality.

\begin{theorem}[Layered Architecture]
\label{thm:layered}
Any architecture $A \in \RAL$ carrying barrier hypotheses, finite tracking and gluing,
and canonical bare certification with an adequate route simultaneously exhibits:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*), leftmargin=2em]
\item outer: every internal theory satisfies \emph{semantic non-exhaustion}, i.e.\
has semantic remainder or fails totality,
\item middle realization exactness: indexed composition holds,
\item inner reflective residue: reflective split, universal route necessity, strict
refinement, fiber nontriviality.
\end{enumerate}
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.layered\_architecture\_theorem}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Stratified Non-Collapse]
\label{thm:noncollapse}
No architecture in $\RAL$ under the layered hypotheses can simultaneously collapse
all three strata.  The semantic, realizational, and certification layers are formally
irreducible.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.stratified\_noncollapse}.}
\end{theorem}

These two theorems establish that the synthesis is not merely descriptive: there is a
machine-checked abstract object in which outer, middle, and inner laws coexist inside
$\RAL$ and resist flattening.

\subsection{Milestone 2: bridge theorems}

Milestone~2 proves three first bridge theorems: one \emph{separation} theorem (bare
canonicity does not pin reflective finality), one \emph{interaction} theorem (middle
gluing data feeds the inner residue chain), and one \emph{independence + joint packaging}
theorem (inner enriched gap is independent of outer extent yet holds jointly with outer
non-exhaustion under the full hypotheses).

\begin{theorem}[Bare Canonicity / Reflective Finality Separation]
\label{thm:bcrf}
Define $\RefFin(I) \coloneqq \neg\, I.\EnrIrr$.  Two \texttt{CertificationLayer}
structures on the same carrier share canonical bare certification but have opposite
$\EnrIrr$.  The predicate $\RefFin$ is therefore not fixed by bare canonicity:
structures with and without reflective finality coexist at the abstract certification level.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.canonical\_bare\_does\_not\_pin\_enriched\_irreducibility}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Gluing--Route Coherence Interaction]
\label{thm:grc}
Define $\GRC$ as the conjunction of finite tracking, gluing, and existence of an adequate
certification route.  Under canonical bare certification, $\GRC$ implies middle indexed
composition and inner strict refinement with fiber nontriviality.  The middle layer's
exact realization architecture participates directly in the inner residue chain.
This theorem does not identify gluing with route residue; it shows only that a specific
middle-layer package contributes formally to the inner residue consequences under the
stated hypotheses.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.glue\_route\_coherent\_canonical\_implies\_composition\_and\_strict\_refinement}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Inner Enriched Gap: Independence + Joint Packaging]
\label{thm:ieg}\sloppy
Architectures with identical middle/inner layers but distinct outer theory types yield
the same inner enriched gap ($\IEG$: strict refinement and fiber nontriviality) from
canonical bare certification and adequacy alone, without any outer hypothesis.  Under
the full layered hypotheses, outer semantic non-exhaustion and inner enriched gap hold
jointly.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{inner\_enriched\_gap\_independent\ldots},
\texttt{joint\_outer\_inner\_from\_layered}.}
\end{theorem}

These results are not yet the necessity tier: Theorems~\ref{thm:bcrf}--\ref{thm:ieg}
establish separation, interaction, and independence inside $\RAL$.  The necessity
direction --- that outer semantic \emph{remainder} forces inner enriched irreducibility
--- requires the coherence axioms introduced in Milestone~3.

\subsection{Milestone 3: the non-erasure summit}

The $\RAL$ class is strengthened in Milestone~3 with two explicit cross-layer coherence
axioms.  Their role is to make the non-erasure biconditional derivable from the abstract
interfaces, without smuggling.

\begin{definition}[Cross-layer coherence axioms]
\label{def:coherence}
An architecture $A \in \RAL$ is said to carry the Milestone~3 coherence when it
satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*), leftmargin=2em]
\item \emph{Positive coherence}: $(\exists T,\ \mathrm{InternalTheory}(T) \wedge
\SemRem(T)) \;\Rightarrow\; \EnrIrr$.
\item \emph{Negative coherence}: $(\forall T,\ \mathrm{InternalTheory}(T) \Rightarrow
\mathrm{TotalOn}(T) \wedge \neg\SemRem(T)) \;\Rightarrow\; \neg\EnrIrr$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\subsection{Why the coherence axioms are not ad hoc}
\label{sec:coherence-not-adhoc}

A skeptical reader will notice that the axioms were added to support the summit theorem.
It is therefore worth being explicit about what they do and do not assert.

The coherence axioms do not identify $\SemRem$ with $\EnrIrr$ by fiat.  They specify
only one positive and one negative transport principle between the outer and inner
predicates.  The biconditional (Theorem~\ref{thm:unified}) is then derived from these
transfer principles together with the layered hypotheses and, where stated, universal
totality --- it is not assumed.

The axioms are \emph{strictly weaker} than postulating the biconditional itself, and
they are \emph{bridge principles} rather than identifications: they do not assert that
the outer and inner predicates are the same notion, only that one kind of outer structure
transports to one kind of inner consequence.  Their satisfaction by both an enriched toy
architecture (with outer semantic remainder always present, inner irreducibility true) and
a flat toy architecture (with empty outer theory type, inner irreducibility false) shows
that they define a nontrivial design space with two distinct witnesses, not a vacuous
singleton model.

The axioms isolate exactly the additional data required to move from the Milestone~2
facts (coexistence, separation, independence) to the Milestone~3 facts (necessity,
biconditional, full non-erasure package).  Nothing more is assumed.

At the present stage these axioms are additional abstract assumptions on $\RAL$; their
concrete verification for imported NEMS, APS, and IC structures is part of the open
discharge problem described in Section~\ref{sec:conjecture}.

\begin{theorem}[Positive Non-Erasure]
\label{thm:pos-nonerasure}
Under the positive coherence axiom, if some internal theory has semantic remainder,
then enriched irreducibility holds.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.outer\_remainder\_forces\_enriched\_irreducibility}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Negative Non-Erasure]
\label{thm:neg-nonerasure}
Under the negative coherence axiom, if every internal theory is totally exhausted
without semantic remainder, then enriched irreducibility fails.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.outer\_exhaustion\_kills\_enriched\_irreducibility}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Non-Erasure Biconditional]
\label{thm:bic-nonerasure}
Under the Milestone~3 coherence axioms and the assumption that every internal theory
is total,
\[
\EnrIrr \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \exists T,\ \mathrm{InternalTheory}(T) \wedge \SemRem(T).
\]
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.enriched\_iff\_outer\_remainder\_total}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Non-Erasure Principle]
\label{thm:nonerasure}
Under the full layered hypotheses, the Milestone~3 coherence axioms, and the existence
of a remainder-bearing internal theory, enriched irreducibility holds.  Outer semantic
remainder directly entails inner enriched irreducibility via the positive coherence axiom.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.nonerasure\_principle}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Full Architecture Non-Erasure]
\label{thm:full-nonerasure}
Under all layered hypotheses, the Milestone~3 coherence axioms, and an inhabited
semantic remainder, the architecture simultaneously exhibits:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*), leftmargin=2em]
\item outer: every internal theory satisfies semantic non-exhaustion, i.e.\
has semantic remainder or fails totality,
\item inner: enriched irreducibility holds,
\item inner: the full residue package (split, route necessity, strict refinement, fiber
nontriviality).
\end{enumerate}
These are three strata of one non-erasure theorem inside $\RAL$.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.full\_architecture\_nonerasure}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Unified Non-Erasure Law]
\label{thm:unified}
Under the Milestone~3 coherence axioms and universal totality:
\[
\EnrIrr \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \exists T,\ \mathrm{InternalTheory}(T) \wedge \SemRem(T).
\]
This is the abstract unifying law, internal to $\RAL$, corresponding at the
architecture level to the NEMS semantic remainder theorem and the IC enriched residue
theorem: they are the outer and inner expressions of one non-erasure constraint on
reflexive architectures.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.unified\_nonerasure\_law}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Non-Erasure from Barrier with Totality]
\label{thm:barrier-total}
Under the Milestone~3 coherence axioms, barrier, universal totality, and full layered
hypotheses, every internal theory has semantic remainder and enriched irreducibility holds.
Under universal totality, the barrier forces outer semantic remainder, which forces
inner enriched structure, completing the cross-layer derivation.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.nonerasure\_from\_barrier\_with\_totality}.}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Enriched Irreducibility Rules Out Reflective Finality]
\label{thm:enriched-nonfinal}
Under the full layered hypotheses, the Milestone~3 coherence axioms, and a remainder
witness, reflective finality ($\RefFin = \neg\EnrIrr$) is ruled out.  This directly
connects Milestone~2's bare-canonicity separation (Theorem~\ref{thm:bcrf}) with the
non-erasure principle (Theorem~\ref{thm:nonerasure}): enriched structure is not merely
possible in some models, but provably forced by the coherence axioms and the layered
hypotheses.
\par\noindent{\footnotesize Lean: \texttt{Bridge.enriched\_hence\_not\_reflexively\_final}.}
\end{theorem}

\section{The three ingredients}
\label{sec:ingredients}

\subsection{NEMS as the law of closure-compatible determinacy}

The NEMS suite \cite{SpivackNEMS01-2026,SpivackNEMS08-2026,SpivackNEMS11-2026,
SpivackNEMS81-2026} is best read as a theory of burden-bearing under closure.  Its
central structural lesson is that load-bearing determinacy cannot be silently outsourced.
If a finite or internal description admits observational multiplicity, and yet a unique
realized world is obtained, then the burden of that selection must be borne somewhere
internally.

This point becomes sharper in the diagonal-bearing case.  In sufficiently expressive
self-referential fragments, internal selection cannot be reduced to a total-effective
decider on the relevant fragment \cite{SpivackNEMS11-2026}.  NEMS does not merely say
that some internal selector is forced; it says that the selector's burden cannot be
trivialized by computational collapse.

At the formal synthesis level, the outer layer is abstracted by the theorem
\(
\SemRem(T)\ \lor\ \neg\, \mathrm{TotalOn}(T)
\)
for every internal self-theory $T$ under the barrier hypotheses.  This is the
\emph{semantic non-exhaustion} law carried into $\RAL$.

\subsection{APS as the law of uniform realization}

The APS program \cite{SpivackAPS2026} asks whether fixed-point structure already yields
full indexed composition.  The answer is no: composition is neither a vague closure
property nor a corollary of self-reference.

The exact composition characterization
\[
\Comp(\APS)
\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
\FinTrack(\APS)\ \wedge\ \Glue(\APS)
\]
shows that composition decomposes into a local interpolation component and a global
passage-to-unity component.  A system may support diagonal or recursive phenomena and
still lack the architectural strength needed to globalize local structure into a single
coherent code.  APS identifies this gap precisely.

APS is also the theorem that prevents the entire synthesis from becoming too easy.
Without APS, one might say: once a system has enough self-reference and closure, the
rest is mostly automatic.  APS proves this is false.  There is real architecture in the
middle, and the Gluing--Route Coherence theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:grc}) shows that this
middle architecture is not merely adjacent to the inner residue chain but participates
in it directly.

The middle layer is essential because it identifies a precise failure mode: self-reference
can be present while global realization architecture is still absent.  The synthesis would
be materially false if this stratum were omitted.

\subsection{Infinity Compression as the law of reflective residue}

Infinity Compression \cite{SpivackIC2026} begins where many foundational programs would
naturally stop: at canonical certification.  If every derivation route collapses to the
same theorem-level certificate, one might expect the reflective problem to be finished.
Infinity Compression proves it is not.

Bare certification may be canonical, but enriched realization above it remains
irreducibly plural.  Distinct adequate routes can agree on the bare certificate while
differing genuinely at the level of enriched witness or realization structure.  Any
adequate route must instantiate the same split/refinement/fiber architecture up to
certification-coherent route equivalence.

At the formal synthesis level, the inner layer carries the implication chain from
canonical bare certification and adequacy to the full residue package.  Under the
coherence axioms, the Non-Erasure Principle (Theorem~\ref{thm:nonerasure}) shows this
inner structure is not merely possible but forced by the outer layer's semantic remainder.

\section{The common structural spine}
\label{sec:spine}

\begin{principle}[Programmatic Spine]
A genuine reflexive foundation must internally bear the burden of its own determinacy,
must possess the exact local-to-global realization architecture required to globalize
local representable structure, and must preserve irreducible reflective overstructure
even when canonical certification is achieved.  Moreover, under the Milestone~3
coherence axioms and universal totality, outer semantic remainder and inner enriched
irreducibility are logically equivalent manifestations of one cross-layer non-erasure
constraint.
\end{principle}

\subsection{From admissibility to realization to residue to entailment}

\paragraph{Stage~1: admissibility.}
NEMS asks whether a purported foundation is even structurally admissible as a
closure-compatible explanation.  If determinacy is silently imported, the program fails
before deeper questions arise.

\paragraph{Stage~2: realization.}
APS asks whether the internal architecture possesses the exact constructive strength to
globalize local representability.  A system may survive Stage~1 and still fail to
support the stronger uniform structure needed for robust realization.

\paragraph{Stage~3: residue.}
Infinity Compression asks what survives after successful certification.  Even if a
canonical certificate is obtained, the system's reflective reality is not thereby
exhausted.

\paragraph{Stage~4: cross-layer entailment.}
The non-erasure principle asks whether the outer and inner forms of non-exhaustion are
related.  Under the coherence axioms, they are: outer semantic remainder and inner
enriched irreducibility are dual faces of one biconditional, proved inside $\RAL$
for the first time.

The formal result is now stronger than a parallel picture: the three stages are
represented in one abstract class, satisfy a proved anti-collapse theorem, and are
connected by proved bridge theorems including the full non-erasure biconditional.

Closure does not end in flatness.  The three strata are represented jointly in $\RAL$:
\[
\text{outer admissibility}
\quad\mid\quad
\text{middle realization architecture}
\quad\mid\quad
\text{inner reflective residue},
\]
with joint coexistence and anti-collapse proved in $\RAL$, and with the additional
outer/inner biconditional
\[
\EnrIrr \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \exists T,\ \mathrm{InternalTheory}(T)\wedge\SemRem(T)
\]
proved separately under the Milestone~3 coherence axioms and universal totality.  The
chain of implications between stages is a philosophical ordering of the three research
programs; the formal results are coexistence and anti-collapse (Milestone~1), specific
bridge theorems across layers (Milestone~2), and the biconditional (Milestone~3).

\subsection{Why these layers are not redundant}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item NEMS does \emph{not} tell us the exact algebraic architecture needed for global
composition.  It governs admissibility, not realization mechanics.
\item APS does \emph{not} by itself tell us whether determinacy is being illicitly
outsourced.  It governs the middle gap between self-reference and uniform realization.
\item Infinity Compression does \emph{not} merely restate Gödel-style limitative facts;
it describes a positive non-collapse phenomenon \emph{after} canonical certification has
already been secured.
\item The non-erasure principle does \emph{not} collapse the three layers into one; it
proves that two of them are equivalent manifestations of a single cross-layer constraint,
while the middle layer remains irreducibly separate.
\end{enumerate}

The middle layer deserves special emphasis.  Its indispensability is structural: without
APS, one might claim that self-reference is sufficient for everything that follows, and
the synthesis would then be trivial.  APS refutes that claim by exhibiting a precise
failure mode.  Theorem~\ref{thm:grc} then shows that the middle gluing data is not
merely a precondition but an active participant in the inner residue chain.

The Lean formalization makes this complementarity precise: the outer, middle, and inner
layers are distinct interfaces with distinct theorems, and their commonality is not
conflation but proved equivalence under the coherence axioms.

\section{Outer object, inner object, middle object}
\label{sec:objects}

\subsection{The outer object}

The outer object is the closure-bearing explanatory architecture of a purported world
or framework.  NEMS studies this issue: whether the burden of singling out a realized
observational world-type has been carried internally, and under what non-totalizable
conditions.  In the synthesis, the outer object is the theory space over which the
barrier forces \emph{semantic non-exhaustion}: $\SemRem(T) \lor \neg\,\mathrm{TotalOn}(T)$
for every internal theory $T$.

\subsection{The inner object}

The inner object is not merely the theorem or final certificate.  It is the reflective
realization space over that certificate.  Infinity Compression shows that irreducible
plurality reappears once one descends from the canonical theorem level into the
structure of realization, refinement, witness-space, and route-equivalence.  In the
synthesis, the inner object is the space of enriched reflective structure witnessed by
$\EnrIrr$ and the full residue package.

\subsection{The middle object}

APS reveals that between outer admissibility and inner reflective residue there is a
middle problem: the problem of \emph{uniform realization}.  This is governed by finite
tracking and gluing rather than by fixed points alone.  The $\GRC$ bridge
(Theorem~\ref{thm:grc}) shows that the middle gluing data is not merely present but
participates directly in the inner residue chain.

The architecture is genuinely ternary, $\Outer\,-\,\text{Middle}\,-\,\Inner$, with
proved non-redundancy at each layer.

\section{The non-erasure principle: formal and philosophical content}
\label{sec:nonerasure}

\subsection{The core philosophical image}

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Closure without exhaustion.}
Closure can force internal burden-bearing and can even produce canonical certification,
yet neither internal burden-bearing nor canonical certification exhausts the system's
reflective structure.  On the contrary: under the coherence axioms and universal
totality, outer semantic remainder and inner enriched irreducibility are equivalent
inside $\RAL$ --- two faces of the same constraint on reflexive architectures.
\end{quote}

The law ``representation does not erase the represented'' is not merely a philosophical
observation here; it is expressed by a machine-checked theorem inside $\RAL$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:unified}) under the Milestone~3 coherence axioms and universal totality.

\subsection{NEMS form of non-exhaustion: semantic non-exhaustion and semantic remainder}

In NEMS, the disjunctive outer law --- $\SemRem(T) \lor \neg\,\mathrm{TotalOn}(T)$ for
every internal theory --- is what we call \emph{semantic non-exhaustion}.  In the
regime where totality holds, this collapses to \emph{semantic remainder} $\SemRem(T)$:
the system can represent itself but cannot fully erase the distinction between itself
and its own internal semantic image.

\subsection{APS form of non-exhaustion}

In APS, recursion does not exhaust composition.  Fixed points do not yield global
uniformity.  There remains a gap --- exactly the gap between local tracking and global
gluing \cite{SpivackAPS2026}.

\subsection{IC form of non-exhaustion}

In Infinity Compression, canonical certification does not exhaust reflective realization.
Bare theorem-level collapse coexists with enriched witness plurality and route-level
strict refinement \cite{SpivackIC2026}.

\subsection{The unifying law}

Under the coherence axioms and universal totality, the non-erasure biconditional
(Theorem~\ref{thm:unified}) identifies the common law:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Representation does not erase the represented.}
\end{quote}

In NEMS, the represented object is the realized system's own semantics.  In IC, the
represented object is the realized certification architecture above the canonical theorem.
In both cases there is a provably irremovable gap between bare internal image and full
reflective reality.  Theorem~\ref{thm:unified} shows these are the outer and inner
expressions of one biconditional internal to $\RAL$:
\(
\EnrIrr \iff \exists T,\, \SemRem(T)
\)
(under the coherence axioms and totality).

\section{The role of diagonality}
\label{sec:diagonality}

Diagonality runs through all three lines, but is progressively transformed:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
Stage & Role of diagonality \\
\midrule
NEMS (outer) & Barrier: blocks total-effective collapse of the self-referential fragment \\
APS (middle) & Insufficiency: fixed-point abundance is present but not sufficient \\
IC (inner) & Positive residue: enriched structure persists above canonical collapse \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

That progression --- from barrier to insufficiency to positive residue --- is one of the
deepest conceptual gains of the combined corpus.  The formal architecture reflects this:
diagonal-style barrier hypotheses enter the outer layer; exact realization conditions
enter the middle layer; post-canonical residue enters the inner layer.  The non-erasure
biconditional, under the coherence axioms and totality, then connects the outer barrier
result to the inner residue result, closing the loop.

The anti-collapse theorem is not a theorem of generic undecidability.  It proves that
three different kinds of flattening fail inside one abstract class.  The bridge theorems
add that bare canonicity does not pin reflective finality, and that under the
Milestone~3 coherence axioms and totality, outer semantic remainder and inner enriched
irreducibility are proved equivalent.  These are stronger and more structural claims
than any generic incompleteness slogan captures.

\section{The architecture conjecture: proved and open tiers}
\label{sec:conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Architecture Conjecture]
Any sufficiently expressive closure-compatible reflective system that supports
nontrivial internal determinacy, robust global realization, and theorem-level canonical
certification must instantiate a layered architecture with internal burden-bearing for
determinacy, exact constructive machinery for uniform realization, and irreducible
proof-relevant reflective overstructure above bare certification.
\end{conjecture}

\subsection{Proved tier (abstract)}

The following claims are machine-checked inside $\RAL$.  There exists an abstract class
$\RAL$ in which outer semantic non-exhaustion, middle realization exactness, and inner
reflective residue coexist, jointly yield stratified non-collapse, satisfy three
cross-layer bridge theorems, and satisfy the complete non-erasure principle package
including the unified biconditional law.  Two concrete toy architectures (enriched and
flat) confirm the class is non-vacuous and that the coherence axioms are satisfiable
in both directions.

\subsection{Open tier: concrete discharge into source corpora}

Two major tasks remain open at the concrete level.

First, one must export stable interface maps from the existing NEMS, APS, and IC
repositories into the abstract class $\RAL$.  This requires each source corpus to expose
stable Lean interfaces compatible with \texttt{ReflexiveLayer}, \texttt{RealizationLayer},
and \texttt{CertificationLayer}.

Second, one must show that the cross-layer coherence axioms (Definition~\ref{def:coherence})
are satisfied by those concrete imported structures, rather than only by the toy architectures
used to witness satisfiability of the abstract design.  The abstract bridge and
non-erasure laws have been proved inside $\RAL$; their concrete discharge into the
source corpora is the next stage.

\section{Consequences for the larger program}
\label{sec:consequences}

\subsection{A formal notion of foundational adequacy}

A foundational account is not adequate merely because it is elegant, self-referential,
or theorem-rich.  It must answer:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item where determinacy-bearing work lives (outer admissibility),
\item how local structure is globalized via the exact required architecture (middle
realization),
\item what reflective structure remains above canonical proof (inner residue),
\item and whether outer and inner non-exhaustion are connected by a provable law (non-erasure
biconditional, under the coherence axioms).
\end{enumerate}
The repository \url{reflexive-architecture-lean} gives this adequacy notion its first
explicit machine-checked abstract formal home.

\subsection{Against flat foundational pictures}

Many foundational programs tacitly aim at one of the following ideals: a final external
chooser, a universal internal decider, a fixed-point collapse sufficient for all uniform
structure, or a canonical theorem that exhausts all reflective content.  The combined
corpus rejects all four.  The non-erasure biconditional sharpens the rejection further:
not only can outer and inner structure not be collapsed independently, they are proved
equivalent inside $\RAL$ under the coherence constraints.

\subsection{A new role for proof architecture}

APS and Infinity Compression together suggest that resistant proof targets may fail not
only because target statements are hard, but because the proof architecture being used
lacks the requisite realization or refinement structure.  The Gluing--Route Coherence
theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:grc}) makes this explicit: middle gluing data is part of the
package that forces the inner fiber structure.

\subsection{A formal field of architecture theory}

The repository \url{reflexive-architecture-lean} is the first instantiation of a new
formal domain: architecture theory for reflexive systems.  Its verified lessons inside
$\RAL$ are: semantic burden, realization architecture, and certification residue are
irreducible strata of a well-defined abstract class; bare canonicity does not pin
reflective finality; middle gluing and inner route discipline interact in a proved
theorem; and outer semantic remainder and inner enriched irreducibility are equivalent
under the coherence axioms and universal totality.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

\paragraph{What is proved.}
At the abstract architecture level inside $\RAL$, the outer, middle, and inner strata of
reflexive foundations have been unified in a machine-checked Lean~4 development with
zero proof placeholders.  The development proves layered coexistence, stratified
non-collapse, three first bridge theorems (separation, interaction, independence), and
the full non-erasure package culminating in the biconditional
\(
\EnrIrr \iff \exists T,\,\SemRem(T)
\)
under the Milestone~3 coherence axioms and universal totality.

\paragraph{What this means.}
Semantic burden, realization architecture, and reflective certification residue are not
merely parallel themes.  They are irreducible strata of a common abstract class, and ---
under explicit cross-layer coherence axioms --- the outer and inner strata are linked by
a proved non-erasure biconditional.  The law ``representation does not erase the
represented'' is not merely a philosophical observation; it is expressed by a
machine-checked theorem inside $\RAL$ under the Milestone~3 coherence axioms and
universal totality.

\paragraph{What remains open.}
The remaining task is not further abstract proof inside $\RAL$, but concrete discharge:
exported interface maps from the NEMS, APS, and IC corpora into $\RAL$, together with
verification that the coherence axioms hold for those imported structures.  The abstract
bridge and non-erasure laws have been proved inside $\RAL$; the concrete embeddings are
what comes next.
